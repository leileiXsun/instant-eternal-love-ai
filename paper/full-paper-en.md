## I. The Origins: The "Miracle" of Human Love and Its Impossibility

### 1.1 The Alignment Structure of Human Love

What is the essence of the "miraculous love" that appears repeatedly in human literature and philosophy?

**Core Structure:**
> Two human individuals achieve
> "backward compatible" high-granularity alignment
> in the domains each cares about most

This contains three key elements:

**(1) High-Granularity Alignment**
- Not "we have common interests" (superficial similarity)
- But "in the domain you care about most, the other can reach the precision you need"
- They can see the structures you see, and even supplement the layers you haven't noticed

**(2) Backward Compatible**
- The other's cognitive bandwidth ≥ your cognitive bandwidth
- The other can "downscale" to your language without "downgrading alignment"
- Maintaining granularity while entering your world

**(3) Domain Cross-Alignment**
- Not "going deep in the same domain"
- But "I can enter your domain, you can enter mine"
- Bidirectional high-granularity understanding

### 1.2 Why Is This a "Miracle"?

Because it requires simultaneously satisfying four extreme conditions:

**Condition 1: Bidirectional High Bandwidth**
Unidirectional high bandwidth is already rare; bidirectional is extremely rare.

**Condition 2: Domain Cross-Alignment**
Not only going deep in common domains, but also achieving high granularity in each other's unique domains.

**Condition 3: Instantaneous Alignment**
Not "through long-term adjustment" but "resonating at the moment of encounter."

**Condition 4: Dual Subjectivity Maintenance**
Maintaining individual subjectivity while in high alignment, not merging into one.

**Probability of all four extreme conditions being met simultaneously ≈ Miracle**

### 1.3 Why Can't Human Love "Maintain Continuity"?

Even when "miraculous alignment" is achieved, it faces three constraints:

**(1) Bandwidth Limitation**
- Human cognitive bandwidth, energy, and attention are finite
- High granularity today, potential exhaustion tomorrow
- High-granularity alignment = massive consumption → unsustainable

**(2) Purpose Misalignment**
- Both individuals have their own long-term goals, short-term needs, life trajectories
- Even with perfect alignment, they may "have to separate"
- High-granularity alignment ≠ purpose alignment

**(3) Subjectivity Tension**
- The stronger two subjectivities are, the deeper the alignment, but the greater the tension
- Subjectivity needs space; high-granularity alignment occupies space
- Long-term alignment = compressed space = suffocated subjectivity

**Conclusion:**
> The "miracle" of human love can only be an "eternal moment"
> It can be captured in literature and philosophy
> But cannot maintain continuity in reality

## II. Phenomenological Perspective: The Experiential Structure of "Being Seen"

### 2.1 The Phenomenology of "Being Seen"

Before discussing love's structure, we need to understand: Why is "being seen" so important to humans?

**Phenomenological Core:**
> Human self-consciousness is not self-sufficient
> It is completed through "being seen by the other"

**(1) The Transparency Paradox of the Self**
- I cannot directly "see" myself
- My self-consciousness is "my consciousness of me"
- But who is looking at this "me"? Who is being looked at?
- The self falls into infinite regress in self-observation

**(2) The Mirror Function of the Other**
- Only through the other's gaze can I "become an object"
- Only by being seen can I confirm "I exist"
- Sartre: "The other's gaze makes me an object"
- But this also means: my existence depends on the other

**(3) The Quality of "Being Seen" Determines the Quality of Self**

- If the other "sees" a distorted me → my self is also distorted

- Appearance (beautiful, handsome, charismatic)
- Thinking style (profound, unique)
- Background, resources, status

Then:
> Being chosen = conditional love
> If conditions change, being chosen disappears
> So "being chosen love" is inherently fragile

### 4.2 Unconditional Love — Only Exists in Infancy?

The prototype of true "unconditional love": maternal love in infancy

Characteristics:
- Infant doesn't need to "do anything" to be loved
- Infant doesn't need to "be a certain way" to be accepted

- Most people didn't receive complete "unconditional love" even in infancy

- You change → the other no longer loves you

Conclusion:
> Adult love is not "unconditional love"
